What is a Social Engineering Attack

There are many forms of Social engineering attacks. Social engineering attacks mostly involve tricking the victim into
giving them access to their system,or they find ways to to get sensitive information on the victim and use it against them

Phishing:

Phishing is when scammers impersonate companies to
try and get you to reveal personal information or opening
software that will give the scammer access to your
computer or device.

Baiting:

Baiting is a method used by scammers to lure the victim into a
trap where they might be able to steal information from the
computer from enticing people with something that sounds
good, an example of which could be downloading more RAM.
Commonly scammers use a USB stick and put it where
people will see it and then when they plug it in, it
automatically installs a virus.

Tailgating:

Tailgating takes place when the scammer uses employees to
get into a restricted area. They ask them to hold the door for
them when they are dressed as a custodian or delivery driver.
When the employee opens the door for them they gain access to
the building that they couldn't get in before, whcih they can
use to get information.

ScareWare:

ScareWare is a form of scamming that scammers use while
threatening the users with a threatening subject such as having a
sensitive video or claiming they have a virus and need to
download software that the scammers abuse. This is relatively
similar to baiting but the scammer threatens the victim instead
of giving them false positives.

Dumpster Diving:

Dumpster diving is when the scammers go through peoples
garbage to uncover sensitive information that wasn't destroyed
in the garbage. They then use this information for their personal
gain.

Quid Pro Quo:

Quid Pro Quo scams take place when scammers requests the user
to give them information or login information in exchange for
supposedly helping their computer in some way. One example of
this scam could be a scammer offering the victim a software that
improves the efficiency of the computer in exchange for
information that benefits the scammer, the scammer in this
situation might call the victim with false promises.
