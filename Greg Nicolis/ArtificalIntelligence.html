Ethics in AI

What are the Ethics in AI?

An ethical AI system is what makes artificial intelligence inclusive, explainable, have a positive purpose and use data responsibly. Without ethics, artificial intelligence wouldn't make sense to the human mind and AI could take over the world. Maybe that's an exaggeration but my point is ethics are critical for artificial intelligence.

Real World Examples and Applications

An ethical AI detects and reduces unfair biases based on race, gender, nationality, etc. Ethics in artificial intelligence are also at your aid for privacy and security. AI systems keep data security at the top. Ethical AI-designed systems provide proper data control and model management systems. Privacy and preserving AI principles help to keep the data secure. Ethical artificial intelligence is also safe as ethics ensure that the AI only does what it's programmed to do and no more. One more way that ethics in AI are useful are for transparency and explainability. Ethical system explains each prediction and output. It provides transparency for the logic of the model. Users get to know the contribution of data for the output. This disclosure justifies the output and builds trust.

Potential benefits and drawbacks

As explained earlier, there are many benefits to making artificial intelligence ethical. It makes AI inclusive of all kinds of people, ensures privacy and safety for your digital property, and is transparent to the user about it's algorithm and potential. There are no drawbacks to ethics in AI, but there are drawbacks to artificial intelligence in itself. To start, AI doing things for us increases human laziness. AI is also expensive to implement. And not only does it increase human laziness but also increases unemployment rate as technologies like this can take over human jobs. AI also lacks creativity, they only do surface level things that they are told and so if they come up with ideas for us, things won't be as creative or unique in the future. AI also cannot understand emotions which can have drawbacks to the potential connection between human and AI. Finally, ethics, as I've been talking about, are very difficult to implement into AI putting your privacy, safety, and many other things at risk.

How AI could affect my future careers

In the future, I either want to be a lawyer, an engineer, or a stock investor. When it comes to bigger jobs like these, I believe AI will actually be beneficial. It can give me tips and complete the easy repetitive parts of the job. The only scenario where AI shouldn't be at aid is for retail jobs like cashiers, waiters, etc. AI could completely take over jobs like this which would be bad since many people work jobs like this and the unemployment rate would skyrocket.
