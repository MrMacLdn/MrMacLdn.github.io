Cyber Security Attacks - Man in the Middle Attacks

Ethics in AI

Ethics are defined as moral principles that govern the conducting of an activity. Examples of AI ethics issues include data responsibility and privacy, fairness, explainability, robustness, transparency, environmental sustainability, inclusion, moral agency, value alignment, accountability, trust, and technology misuse.

Techniques and Methods used

Cyber criminals use internet protocol spoofing to gain access to devices and sensitive information. IP spoofing happens when cyberattackers alter the source IP address of a website, email address, or device to mask it.
Cyber criminals use domain name system spoofing to alter domain names and redirect users to fake websites operated by cybercriminals. This tricks users into thinking they are on a secure and trusted website.

Measures and practices to defend against attacks

Organizations should employ encryption standards to protect data in use or at rest. For data shared into, within, and out of an organization, encryption of the data at the file and volume levels is requisite. This helps ensure that cybercriminals and rogue nation-states are not able gain access to the data, even if they gain some level of access.

The Impact of Man in the Middle Attacks

Once a criminal has gained information about users, they can manipulate account credentials, steal funds, or make unauthorized purchases.
