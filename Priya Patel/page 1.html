Definitions


An insider threat can be defined
as a cyber security risk that comes from
people within an organization. They use their
authorized access to negatively impact the
organization's resources,network, systems, etc.










Insider threat techniques

The first insider threat technique is data theft. Taking pictures of private documents and sending emails to private accounts are ways this can occur. Hacking, financial gain, and revenge are all reasons as to why this can happen. The next technique is privilege escalation. By getting privileges to use and abuse private resources, this can occur. The next technique is sabotage. This can occur from the desire for revenge or blackmail purposes. Instead of stealing data, their objective is to occur or delete it. The three types of insider threats are, malicious insiders,compromised insiders, and negligent insiders. Malicious insider threats harm the integrity of the company. Negligent insider threats are when an individual within the organization puts sacred information at risk, as a result of a lack of attention, human error, or disregarded security protocols. Compromised insider threats refers to people within an organization who had their access compromised stolen by outside l threat actors. Employees, contractors, or business partners may misuse important data, which can cause harm to the organization in many ways.

Real life examples

The first real life example of this attack is when an employee who was leaving Yahoo stole trading secrets. This happened in May of 2022, when he stole information about Yahoo's new Adlearn product. He downloaded around 570,000 pages of Yahoo's ip to his own devices. The information he had would have been enough to give competitors a great advantage. The next real life example is the departing google employee who bought data of the company back in 2016. He downloaded thousands of files of Google's self- driving program onto his personal devices, in hopes that it will give him an advantage for his uber job. The last real life example is the insider at BGupa who stole and sold company data on the black web, for his own financial gain. This happened back in 2017. He got all the information he needed on a CRM, and then sold it on the black market, to about 550, 000 people. Lastly, is the insider at Boering who sent private company information to a personal email. He forwarded a spreadsheet that contains private information about the company, to his wife in hopes that she can help him with formatting. In the hidden column it had about 36,000 of his co-workers' personal information.

Impact

The impact insider threats have on individuals is that it can lead to feelings of mistrust and stress. If the threat involved a friend or coworker they trusted, this can lead to a strain in people's relationships. The loss of trust they feel can cause job dissatisfaction. Insider attacks can lead to personal data being compromised, which can lead to identity theft or cause an individual to experience significant financial loss. If someone's personal information like financial and medical records are stolen, this can lead to identity theft. The stress that comes with this, can lead to financial distress and excessive stress. Insider thre­ats can harm society by eroding trust and compromising security. Whe­n individuals abuse their authorized acce­ss to sensitive information or systems, it can re­sult in breaches across national security, public safe­ty, and critical infrastructure. Such repercussions e­xtend far beyond immediate­ consequences, impacting the­ stability and proper functioning of society as a whole. Additionally, this misconduct has the­ potential to undermine public confide­nce in institutions and systems establishe­d to safeguard and support communities.Insider thre­ats pose a considerable risk to organizations, le­ading to both financial and reputational harm. When insiders with malicious inte­nt steal intellectual prope­rty, trade secrets, or se­nsitive business information, their compe­titors gain an unfair advantage. As a consequence­, affected organizations can suffer significant financial losse­s, lose market share, and e­ven face bankruptcy. Additionally, insider thre­ats bring negative publicity and erode­ trust in the organization, making it more challenging to attract custome­rs and partners.

Measures for this attack

A key me­asure in preventing and addre­ssing insider threats is impleme­nting robust access controls. This entails granting access to spe­cific information or systems solely to those individuals who re­quire it for their roles. By doing so, unauthorize­d parties are preve­nted from accessing sensitive­ data, effectively minimizing the ­ risk of insider threats. Regularly monitoring and auditing syste­m activity is another effective­ measure. This means ke­eping a close eye­ on employees' compute­r activities and watching for any suspicious or abnormal behavior. By actively monitoring and auditing, it be­comes easier to ide­ntify signs of insider threats and take prompt action to pre­vent any potential harm.
