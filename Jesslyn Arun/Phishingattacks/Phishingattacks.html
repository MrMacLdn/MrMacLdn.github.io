Phishing Attacks

Phishing is when hackers steal sensitive information like password, username, credit card information, etc. The attacker pretends to be a reputable source and lure in victims. It is kind of like when fishermen catch fishes so that is where the name comes from. It is really cheap and easy so they are really common.

Techniques Used in Phishing Attacks

Some of the common phishing techniques are advanced-fee scam, account deactivation scam, website forgery scam. Advanced-fee scam is when an attacker sends an email saying they will give you lots of money if you give a small amount of money upfront. Account deactivation scam is when an attacker says your account will be soon deactivated if you don’t take action. They will then ask you for your username and password. Website forgery scam is when the attacker makes a website that looks trustworthy and you have to put in your sensitive information which will be for sale or other use.

Real-life Example

Fischer Advanced Composite Components AG is an Austrian aeronautics company. In 2016, an employee got an email pretending to be the company’s CEO. The employee then transferred almost 50 million euros. The company was able to recover 10 million euros but the rest was lost.

Impact on Individuals Organizations and Society

Attackers can affect a business by installing malware or ransome. It can disrupt the system and cause system outages. It can get sensitive information. It can be really difficult to combat. Businesses can lose millions of dollars. Attackers can affect individuals by installing malware, sabotage systems,etc which can hurt their credit score and even ruin their reputation. It can lead to identity theft, financial loss, data breaches, etc. Attackers can affect a society by violating trust. It can cause depression and anxiety. It can make people feel helpless.

Protect Against Phishing Attacks

You can protect against phishing attacks by never providing your personal information like your account number, password, social security number, financial information, etc over the phone or the internet if it is not a trusted source. You should not click on any suspicious emails or links.
